On 19 February 2013 11:08, Manuel González Suárez <manuelteral at gmail.com> wrote:
> Hi all
> My name is Manuel Gonzalez and I am a professor of Classical Greek at the
> University of Oviedo (Spain). I am preparing a critical edition in which I
> want to include several indices (of names, of passages cited, etc..) The
> question is, the procedure is the same as in LaTeX, ie \ newindex, etc.?
> Thanks and sorry for my poor English.
> Manuel

I don't suppose you will encounter any problems if you use the general
index commands. But if you want to index your critical text, as
opposed to your own commentary or introduction, then you have the
\edindex{} option, which gives you additional access to line numbers.
See page 28 of eledmac's documentation:

"LaTeX provides the \index{ item } command for specifying that item
and the current page number should be added to the raw index (idx)
file. The \edindex{item} macro can be used in numbered text to specify
that item and the current page & linenumber should be added to the raw
index file."

Hope this helps,
Arash

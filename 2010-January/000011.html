>> I'm typesetting an edition where we decided that we want to print two
>> manuscript-pages per printed page, as the ms has a rather small
>> format. The line-breaks are supposed to agree with the
>> manuscript's. The only problem is that this renders the apparatus
>> ambigous, as every linenumber apprears twice per page. Thus I'd like

>> apparatus.

> I'm sorry not to have a complete solution to your problem.  But as no

Forget about what I wrote yesterday.  You definitely don't want to use
columns.  I did that once, and it was suitable for my purpose, but is
not for yours.

Maybe minipages are a solution to your problem, but I never worked with
the corresponding feature of ledmac.  Do the footnotes get printed on
the real page or on the minipage?  If they only get printed on the
minipage, you have to do more work.

I'm not sure if I would do this with ledmac anyway, as you don't rely on
TeX's linebreaking and ledmac's feature of line numbering and computing
corresponding notes.  You might consider doing your typesetting in
minipages (or \vboxes), placing them together with a hard written (on a
per page base) footnote apparatus.

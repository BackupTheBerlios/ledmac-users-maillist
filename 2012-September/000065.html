Dear Maïeul,

As always, I'm deeply grateful to you for all the work you are doing on
ledmac.

I think the fork of a new ledmac release with a new name will definitely
help users who have created complex documents that depend on the ledmac
macros as they existed from 2003 until 2012/08/03 (v0.17).

I think your additions and updates to ledmac since 2012/08/03 will
significantly help users who are embarking on new editions, as well as
those who have the TeX skills to modify their old style files to match the
new macro functions.

While the name "Ledmac1" achieves the minimal work of being a new name,
wouldn't a snazzier name like "LedmacX" or something be more obvious, fun
and attractive?  What do others think?

Best, and thanks again,

Dominik
<http://wujastyk.net/pgp.html>



On 10 September 2012 17:04, Maïeul Rouquette <maieul at maieul.net> wrote:

> Dear Friend,
>
> talking with Dominiki about the break compatibility in Ledmac, I agreed
> with his idea to provide 2 versions of ledmac/ledpar.
>
> 1) Ledmac and Ledpar 0, wich won't be maintened anymore. The version 0.19
> will be corresponding with the old version 0.17, because the break
> compatibility of overload was made on 0.18
> 2) Version 1.0. The main idea of this version is that people should'nt
> have to overload any "major" macro of ledmac, because
>         1) They should'nt have to be a hacker.
>         2) This technic is dangerous for compatibility descendant, because
> it implies no change in the API.
>
> But as you know, it's not possible in LaTeX to specify which version of
> package we Load.
>
> That is why, the version 1 will be distributed by the CTAN with an other
> name : ledmac1 and ledpar1 (ledarab will be unmaintend too, because I think
> we should RTL possibility of moderne processeur).
>
> The manual of the version 0.1 will invite to use version 1 instead. For
> people who didn't hack ledmac (i.e how did'nt made their own footnote
> series of formating), it will be transparent. For people who hacked, they
> should make some adaptation, but they will have a more clean code.
>
> I think I will print a error message in the version 0.x which invite to
> use version 1.x instead, or to add a command to hide this message.
>
> The manual of ledmac 1.0 will suppress all overload description, which can
> be problematic. I will try to implement some hook instead of these
> overload. I think I will have only one : the font family for lemma and
> footnote, to have ≠ font in ≠ series.
>
> I hope to publish one beta 4 this week, to publish official version this
> week-end.
>
> I ask for your opinion.

Hello list,

On variant collecting:  I have typed in my starter text from the "best" manuscript and I first do a quick clean up for spelling and things that are obvious.  Then I go through word by word and check each manuscript, including rechecking the one that the starter text was based off of, and I enter the variants.  Luckily for this edition I am only using 3 manuscripts, so I have the scans of them all open at once in the top part of my screen.  I usually will do five or ten verses for each manuscript before switching to the next, as that keeps it a bit interesting, but I don't get too far ahead either.  Sometimes a variant in another manuscript prompt me to go back and recheck my transcription of a previous reading, so not going too far ahead helps make that quicker.  And of course with manuscripts letters occasionally look ambiguous and what you actually decide the manuscript reads will depend on what you think it should read in some instances.  For the really ambiguous letters I use special brackets to indicate uncertainty.

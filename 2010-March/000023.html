*After* you defined your contents -- either in the pages- or in the pairs-environment -- you still have to trigger the output itself. For parallel pages this is done by the command 
\Pages
for parallel columns (cf p. 5 of the docs)
\Columns


So if you write

> \end{pairs}
\Columns
> \end{document}


your code typesets just fine. 

You also might want to remove the \chapter{} from the numbered text, as you won't get any good result. Sectioning-commands are a problem in ledmac in general.
I haven't worked with poery yet, but for prose, I've usually only "faked" the appearance of headings with formating commands. If somebody has a better way to do this, I'd love to hear about it though...

> 
> Seeing that ledmac/ledpar has a new maintainer, who has quickly found the time to fix an irritating bug (thanks for that, Maïeul!) I see my chance to propose some fixes, improvements and additions.
> 
> The reason why I don't add them directly to github is that I am hoping for some input from you other users on these proposals: 
> 	Do you agree that these would be good additions? 
> 	Which ones would you prioritise? (below my own tentative ranking)
> 	Do you have any good workarounds for the problems I describe?
> 
> 
> Thanks for reading/thinking about/commenting!
> All the best,
> Florian.
> 
> 
> A: BUGS (?):
> 
> 1) Large apparatuses (more than halv the page) makes ledpar go out of sync after some pages.
> 2) when enlarging the page with a flexible length e.g.
> \addtolength{\skip\Afootins}{-2.5mm} 
> but only using the footnoteseries A on *one* side of ledpar, the syncing gets messed up.
> When I use a fixed length instead, e.g.
> \setlength{\skip\Afootins}{5mm}
> the behaviour seems to be ok.
> 
> 
> B: SUGGESTIONS FOR IMPROVEMENTS:
> 
> 1) BETTER PAGEBREAK CONTOL: 
> In the main text I can use \pagebreak in between paragraphs (\pend\pagebreak\pstart) in ledmac, but it is ignored inside a paragraph and completely ignored everywhere in ledpar. I'd really like \pagebreak to work like in standard LaTeX.
> Footnotes are never broken over page-breaks. Usually this is a good practice, but sometimes, when they get very long (e.g. when one ms adds or replaces a whole paragraph or even chapter) one needs to split them over several pages. Also here a sort of specialised \pagebreak-command would come in very handy.
> (Can be worked around manually, but VERY cumbersome.)
> 
> 2) WIDOW/ORPHAN CONTROL in ledpar. Without 1) it even doing this manually is very difficult.
> 
> 3) SECTIONING COMMANDS (\chapter/\section…) as part of the numbered text. 
> This applies mainly when editing texts that already have sectioning, that I need to reproduce and possibly add critical remarks to. 
> Ideally ledmac would have two series of sectioning-commands: The usual ones, that *automatically* stop line-numbering, and a second one, \edchapter, \edsection, … that have the same formatting as the usual ones, write to leftmark/rightmark, add TOC-entries, *but* are part of the numbered text.
> (Can be worked around manually, but cumbersome.)
> 
> 4) \LEDSIDENOTES WORKING EVERYWHERE, also in (i.e. beside) the apparatus. Cf. e.g. the marginnotes-package.
> 
> 5) allow LINE BREAKS AND INDENTS IN THE APPARATUS.
> 
> 6) \ledsidenotes shouldn't just be deleted when the next ledsidenote happens to be in the same row – there should either be a warning and/or automatic pushing them to the next line, preferably both.
> 
> 7) draft-mode: I know, it can be changed easily, but italicising is IMO an unfortunate default, as it is used in a number of other contexts (many critical texts use it for abbreviations) and it changes spacing; underlining works reasonably well.
> 
> 
> 
> 
> C: THINGS I'D LOVE TO SEE SOMETIMES IN THE FUTURE:
> 
> 1) more than two text-streams for editions with more than two main text versions.
> 
> 2) possibility to put the apparatus completely on one side, the text on the other (cf. newer vols. of Corpus Scriptorum Ecclesiasticorum Latinorum)
